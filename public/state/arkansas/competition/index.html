A high competition grade means state legislative elections are often contested, the winners’ margin of victory is narrow, and seats are not considered safe (i.e., the winner’s vote share is not greater than 55% of the vote). . To calculate the grade, raw competition scores for each state are calculated (see below). These are combined across years, and weighted so more recent years count more.


Each state’s raw score in a given year is composed of four elements: the average vote share of election winners, the average margin of victory, the percentage of races that are uncontested, and the portion of legislative seats won with a large margin of victory (10% or more). This number is called the state’s HVD score (for political scientists Thomas Holbrook and Emily Van Dunk).
